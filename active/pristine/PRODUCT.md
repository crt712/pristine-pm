## Fragrance library (canonical)
Studio-developed, perfume-grade compositions; intended for cloning/scale evaluation and **controlled integration across system stages** (per archive). Fragrance is **modulation** in the system, not the core claim.

| Name | Composition notes (archive) | Structural position (factual) | System role (operational) |
|------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Fleur Blanche** | Bergamot, lime, basil, rice flower, orchid, amber | Citrus top; basil; white floral mid (rice flower, orchid); amber base | Fresh-floral modulation; restrained sweet character |
| **Linen Noir** | Patchouli, cedar, amber, lavender, lemon, coriander, orange blossom, citrus zest, moss, sandalwood | Patchouli and moss; cedar and sandalwood; lemon and zest; lavender and coriander; orange blossom; amber | Wood- and earth-forward profile with controlled citrus and aromatic lift |
| **Pure Haven** | Driftwood, vetiver, sea salt, oud, bergamot | Bergamot opening; sea salt; driftwood and vetiver; oud | Marine-wood profile; mineral and wood emphasis; not sweet-aquatic |

### Archive governance (factual constraints)
- Compositions: studio-developed; perfume-grade; supplied for cloning and scale testing.
- Design intent (archive): support system architecture without dominating it; fragrance does not replace preservation/mechanism framing.
- Pack / positioning constraints stated in archive: avoid scent-led or indulgent claims; no “luxury scent” positioning; no lifestyle-theatrics framing on pack.
