## Purpose

Explore how customer acquisition cost (CAC), pricing, and repeat behavior influence overall business viability.

This simulation uses directional ranges only and does not represent validated inputs.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):
- Low: $15
- Mid: $25
- High: $40

Retail price:
- $18
- $22
- $26

Repeat behavior (qualitative, not numeric):
- Low repeat (infrequent reorder)
- Moderate repeat
- High repeat (strong retention)

## Derived Relationships (observational only)

This simulation evaluates:

- Payback behavior (how quickly CAC is recovered)
- Sensitivity to repeat purchases
- Interaction between price and acquisition cost
- Dependence on retention for viability

Do not compute exact LTV or full tables.

---

## Scenario Observations (directional)

- Higher CAC increases dependence on repeat behavior
- Lower price points require stronger retention to recover acquisition cost
- High CAC + low repeat creates the most fragile growth scenarios
- Higher pricing can offset CAC but may introduce conversion pressure

Do not recommend strategies or select optimal outcomes.

## Key Questions

- At what combination of CAC and repeat behavior does the model become unsustainable?
- How dependent is viability on retention vs pricing?
- What breaks first: acquisition economics or customer willingness to pay?
