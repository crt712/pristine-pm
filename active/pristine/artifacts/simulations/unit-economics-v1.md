## Key Insights (Session 1)

- Dose acts as a compounding pressure variable that affects both margin and cost-per-load perception
- High COGS combined with high dose creates the most fragile economic scenarios
- Lower-dose structures appear more resilient across COGS variability
- Pricing provides margin buffer, but cannot fully offset unfavorable COGS + dose combinations

---

## Key Insights (Session 2 — Dose Sensitivity)

- Dose behaves as a multiplier on economic pressure rather than a simple input variable
- Dose sensitivity appears nonlinear, with higher-dose ranges creating disproportionate economic fragility
- There exists a high-dose "fragility zone" where margin resilience and value perception both degrade
- Dose directly impacts customer perception of efficiency, not just underlying margin
- Lower-dose structures improve overall system stability across COGS and pricing variability

---

## Key Insights (Session 3 — Real-World Behavior)

- Real-world dose variability introduces a persistent and compounding economic risk even under otherwise stable assumptions
- Small, repeated overuse ("just to be safe" behavior) acts as a hidden fragility mode that is difficult to detect early
- Economic stability depends not just on intended dose, but on how closely real users adhere to dosing guidance
- Faster-than-expected product depletion can shift customer perception from "efficient system" to "high-consumption product"
- The system must remain stable under imperfect dosing, not just ideal usage conditions
