## Dose system behavior (insight only)

- Dose acts as a multiplier on economic pressure rather than a simple input
- Dose sensitivity is nonlinear, with higher-dose ranges creating disproportionate fragility
- Real-world usage will likely drift upward ("just to be safe" behavior)
- Economic stability depends on tolerance to imperfect dosing, not ideal use
- Faster depletion can shift perception from "efficient system" to "high-consumption product"

**Status:** Insight only (not yet validated in real-world testing). Should inform formulation discussions and testing design.

---

## Retention-dependent economics (insight only)

- Business viability is structurally dependent on repeat behavior rather than first-purchase economics
- High CAC can be tolerated only if repeat behavior is strong; low repeat creates immediate fragility
- Pricing provides margin support but cannot compensate for weak retention
- Retention is influenced not just by performance, but by perceived efficiency and product longevity
- Dose behavior and product consumption rate may directly impact repeat behavior and therefore overall viability

**Status:** Insight only (not yet validated in real-world acquisition or retention data). Should inform product design (dose tolerance) and future go-to-market expectations.

---

## System-level model (product → retention → viability) (insight only)

- Dose behavior influences consumption rate and perceived efficiency
- Perceived efficiency shapes customer value perception and trust
- Value perception drives repeat behavior and retention quality
- Retention determines whether customer acquisition cost is recoverable
- Business viability is therefore dependent on a continuous chain from product use → perception → retention → economics
- The system must remain stable under imperfect real-world usage, not ideal conditions
- Failure can occur through behavioral drift (overuse), even if product performance is strong
- Retention is not purely a marketing outcome; it is directly influenced by product experience and usage patterns
- Viability degrades fastest when upward dose drift combines with high CAC and weak repeat behavior
- Acquisition economics and product-use behavior are coupled through retention, meaning changes in usage behavior can directly impact CAC recovery
- Stability requires resilience to imperfect usage conditions because behavioral drift can amplify CAC pressure over time

**Status:** System-level insight (not yet validated in real-world testing). Should inform formulation, testing design, and future go-to-market expectations.
