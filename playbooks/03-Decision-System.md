# Decision System

Last updated: 2026-03-25

## Purpose
Make decisions by **creating clarity**, not waiting for certainty.

## When to use
- When you're stuck or overthinking
- Before commitments with meaningful cost or lock-in
- When you need to pick an approach under uncertainty

## Core philosophy
- Move with **enough information**, not perfect information
- Reduce ambiguity before deciding
- Make risk visible; decide intentionally
- Treat decisions as hypotheses; validate with action
- Adjust without ego (progress > being right)

## Decision model
**Clarify → Frame → Decide → Validate → Adjust**

### 1) Clarify the decision
- What decision is actually being made?
- What outcome are we trying to achieve?
- What does success look like?
- If not clear: don't decide yet.

### 2) Frame the options
- Reduce to real choices (often A / B / do nothing)
- Avoid endless options and complexity

### 3) Decide using what matters
Variables:
- Impact
- Reversibility
- Cost (time/money/energy)
- Speed required

### 4) Validate through action
- Define what you'll do next and what signals you'll watch

### 5) Adjust without ego
- Update based on evidence, not attachment

## Speed rules
- **Reversible** → decide quickly; learn
- **Hard to reverse** → add structure, not delay
