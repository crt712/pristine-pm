# Partners & Vendors

Last updated: 2026-03-25

## Purpose
Choose operating partners that improve outcomes long-term and avoid expensive partnership mistakes.

## When to use
- Any vendor/partner selection with meaningful dependency, cost, or risk
- Anytime you're tempted to pick the "fast/easy/cheap" option

## Core philosophy
Optimize for **reliable execution + long-term alignment**, not lowest price or fastest path.

## Evaluation model
**Credibility → Capability → Fit → Risk → Decision**

### 1) Credibility (Can I trust them?)
- Legit, experienced, responsive, professional

### 2) Capability (Can they do this?)
- Relevant experience; understands nuances; can deliver

### 3) Fit (Will this work long-term?)
- Aligned operating style; collaborative; clear communication

### 4) Risk (What could go wrong?)
- Failure points, dependencies, lock-in, reversibility

### 5) Decision
- Good enough to proceed? Upside > risk? Trust sufficient?

## What to weight most (early signals)
- **Responsiveness** (strongest filter)
- **Clarity of communication**
- **Thoughtfulness** (asks good questions, thinks ahead)

## Red flags
- Slow/inconsistent responses; vague answers; avoids direct questions
- Overpromising; lack of detail; missed commitments
- Defensive when questioned; transactional vibe; low collaboration
- Doesn't understand the business; misaligned incentives; short-term thinking

## Pilot-first rule ("Earn the next level of trust")
Don't go all-in immediately.
- Start with smaller scope
- Test responsiveness + quality
- Earn expanded scope through observed behavior

## Decision speed
- Move faster when commitment is low and switching is easy
- Slow down (structure, not stall) when cost is high or dependency is long-term

## Operating after selection
Selection is the beginning.
- Set clear expectations (scope, timelines, quality bar)
- Maintain structured communication
- Observe behavior over time; adjust structure accordingly

## Exit strategy
If needed:
- Reduce scope
- Transition out
- Move on cleanly (no drama)
