# Relationships & Network

Last updated: 2026-03-25

## Purpose
Treat relationships as long-term assets, not short-term transactions.

## When to use
- Building new relationships with high-leverage people
- Maintaining relationships without being intrusive
- Activating your network for a specific ask

## Core philosophy
- Build trust over time
- Stay top of mind without friction
- Be helpful and thoughtful
- Maintain relationships even when there's no immediate need

Rule:
- Don't chase. Create lightweight, consistent touchpoints.

## Relationship model
**Build → Maintain → Activate**

### Stage 1 — Build
Goal: strong first impression + credibility
- Intentional, context-rich outreach
- No generic messages
- Show prior thinking

### Stage 2 — Maintain
Goal: stay relevant without annoyance
- Low pressure, natural timing
- Value-driven touchpoints
- No forced check-ins

### Stage 3 — Activate
Goal: engage with purpose when needed
- Clear ask + relevant context
- Easy to respond to

## High-value relationship strategy
High value:
- Key partners (labs, co-packers, advisors)
- Strategic operators
- People with unique knowledge/leverage
- Long-term aligned people

Operating rules:
- More intentional touchpoints (quality > frequency)
- Context-rich updates (progress-triggered)
- Reconnect ~every 4–8 weeks based on meaningful updates
- Higher quality asks (specific, worth their time)
- Build continuity (reference prior conversations)

## Boundaries + deprioritization signals
Signals:
- Repeated non-responsiveness
- Low engagement
- One-sided effort

Response:
- Reduce effort and frequency
- Keep door open
- No confrontation
