# Founder Operating System (Execution Layer)

Last updated: 2026-03-25

## Purpose
Operate as a founder by doing what **moves things forward**, not what feels productive.

## When to use
- Weekly planning
- When you feel scattered or overwhelmed
- When multiple workstreams compete for attention

## Definition of progress (critical)
Progress is not activity. Progress is something that **meaningfully advances the business**.

Examples:
- Product closer to launch
- Clarity on a key decision
- Partner relationship advanced
- Risk validated or eliminated
- Positioning/messaging improved

Filter:
- "Does this actually move something forward?"

## Operating model
**Think → Focus → Execute → Sync → Adjust**

- **Think (clarity first)**: step back and define what matters
- **Focus (select what matters)**: choose 1 primary + 1–2 secondary priorities
- **Execute (move forward)**: momentum > perfection
- **Sync (reassess reality)**: what changed, what's true now?
- **Adjust (stay adaptive)**: shift effort based on reality

## Managing multiple workstreams
Rule: Don't balance everything equally.

At any given time:
- **1 primary**
- **1–2 secondary**
- Everything else: **background or paused**

## Weekly rhythm (light, not rigid)
- **Start of week**: what matters most? what makes this week successful?
- **Midweek**: am I moving the right things forward or getting pulled into noise?
- **End of week**: what actually moved? what didn't? what changes next week?

Template:
- `templates/weekly-priorities-template.md`

## Common traps (and fixes)
- Busy work → always tie work to real progress
- Overbuilding too early → validate before perfection
- Spreading too thin → prioritize aggressively
- Waiting for perfect clarity → move with enough clarity
